Established in 2004 by Jo Broad, KindleCo is a specialist  independent service 'supporting the supporters' of Children, Young People & Families to thrive in a landscape of change ....

"we are living in times of Revolutionary change....for which there are no precedents...our children are confronting challenges that are unique in human history" (Sir Ken Robinson, Out of Our Minds, 2018)
 

My work is impact-driven and values-led 

My values are to work with four enduring principles

Collaboration    Compassion    Courage    Creativity

Through KindleCo, I strive to build knowledge, capacity & capability in organisations, in their strategic partnerships, their stakeholder networks and in the individual professionals delivering against the canvass of public policy, by supporting:

  Organisational development - strategy creation, implementation

  Policy response - knowing the external landscape -  understanding, shaping, deploying 

  Projects - Planning, Direction & Coaching for multi-stakeholder work

  Partnerships & Resources - development, brokerage, positioning, funding, collaborating

  Systems - thinking, mapping, analysing, co- design

I share experience built over more than 25 years of work with young people themselves and in the brokerage, development and design of multi-organisation programmes.

I strive to bring knowledge, insights and ideas to the table and work with you to develop practical solutions to your challenges. 

What makes KindleCo different to other Consultancies?  My contribution to your work will be policy-informed & practice-sensitive. I specialise in partnering organisations who advocate for, or deliver opportunities in, Learning, Skills development, Wellbeing including Mental Health and Creativity to promote the lifechances of their end-beneficiaries

I am at my most professionally passionate when working in key Public Policy areas of              Social Inclusion    Learning    Mental Health    the Arts

I am committed to the value of collaboration in Social Purpose work, an advocate for equality of opportunity, a lover of systems-change thinking and a believer in the transformative power of creativity

On this site you will find a pick & mix of project profiles, client case studies and professional history which seek to reflect this approach and those values.

A little more about Jo ....

My commitment to developing the skills and lifechances of Children & Young People is longstanding, along with my belief in advocacy as an agent of change. 
 
Following a decade or so working on national scale projects at local and regional agency level, including through Cultural Services and the Creative Industries, I have focused my attention in more recent years exclusively on work which supports organisations delivering in complex stakeholder environments for the benefit of Families. Learning, Mental Health and SEND have become a focus whilst Creativity and the Arts remain close to my heart. 
 

I hold a Certificate from EMCC in Supervision of Consultants, Coaches and Mentors in the workplace.

Want to know more about my qualifications and love of CPD? Visit my LinkedIn page or read on...

I originally trained as a Music Practitioner.  It sounds an odd connection to what I do now, granted, but actually those disciplines have much in common - team work, coaching for performance, knowing the internal and external environment. Making the 'bigger picture' more than the sum of its individual parts. Orchestras are communities where hard work, skill, compassion, respect and diversity are the lifeblood. Collaboration matters.

I made a move to Local Authority working in the 1990s as part of a Family Learning team promoting the lifechances of our most vulnerable children. I have not left the public sector scene since, in one way or another. It and its network of partners, in the Charitable, Social Enterprise and Voluntary sectors, make it a vibrant place to engage with the most committed professionals you will ever meet. The way we do business and interact has radically altered but the dynamics of change, influencing, collaboration and of shared purpose are still built on the quality of Relationships. 
 
I am buoyed on by the belief that we all have an opportunity, to create positive impact on the lifechances of Children & Young People......
