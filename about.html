The job of looking ahead, understanding the external environment, preparing for change and moving ahead has never been more challenging for the Public Sector & their Social Purpose partners. The nature, pace and scope of societal change is eye-watering. That experienced in a Government-influenced working environment is can be overwhelming.
But it can also be full of opportunity.
Founded by Jo Broad in 2004, KindleCo is a specialist partner to Public Sector & Social Purpose organisations occupied with sector-level change and their own development.
Through KindleCo, Jo commits to deliver work which is values - led:
"Collaboration   Compassion    Courage   Creativity"
"I'd like to invite you on a journey to build greater meaning, resilience and impact into your work, strengthen the outcomes for your beneficiaries and its contribution to systems-change" 
Whether it is winning #stakeholder buy-in, enabling inter-agency collaboration, galvanising political will or unlocking investment, we all have the opportunity to make an impact for Children & Young People.

With skills honed over more than  25 years of working directly with Children & Young People and with the agencies which champion them, Jo facilitates, supports & enables 

Organisations , Strategic Partnerships , Networks  & Consortia  

working in a Government - influenced business environment to develop, collaborate and evaluate -  to respond confidently to external change by building their own capacities for:

Policy insight

Strategy creation 

Collaboration 

Systems thinking & expression

Reflection in evaluation

Working in the key Public Policy areas of

Social Inclusion, Lifelong Learning, Education & Skills, Mental Health & Wellbeing, Creativity & the Arts

Expertise

Through KindleCo, I share experience gained over the past 25+ years of working in & with the Public Sector at local, regional and sector level

I understand the challenges and the joys of

inter-agency working, stringent funding criteria, complex stakeholder dynamics

I trained initially as a Musician, spending many years coaching children, young people & adults in schools, extended service settings and privately, working towards public performances. I furthered my own professional development with a BMus. Hons in #Conducting, with a dab of Concert Management, Orchestration and Early Childhood Musical Literacy thrown in for good measure!

I entered the realms of  working alongside the Public Sector in 1998 when I took on the artistic programming and business development of a community-led venture in West London, developing its first ever Outreach Programme for Arts & Community Education.

It wasn't long before I was bitten by the Strategy bug, and soon became a fan of multi-agency working, prompting a move to St Helens Council in Merseyside in 1998 to be their first ever Arts Officer. Boyed on by their incredible commitment to Learning as a tool for Social Inclusion & Economic Regeneration, I established the towns first ever cross-agency Cultural Partnership and with it, a first Creative Industries business incubation programme for Young People.

It was also a period when we managed to persuade the powers that be to fund a new Youth Theatre, Disability Arts Programme and contribute to the Day in the Dome event marking the Millennium - taking 50 of the regions brightest young talents to London to perform with successful bids to the Standards Fund, Arts Council and European ERDF bids via Merseyside ACME, lighting the way.

Combining experience of developing strategy in multi-agency contexts, spring-boarding new projects in the Public Sector and life as a former Orchestra Coach, I delivered a stint at Arts Council England as their Regional Officer for Lifelong Learning & Skills in the early 2000s. This allowed me to stretch my situational Leadership legs, leading the construction with stakeholders, of ACE East's new three-year Learning & Skills strategy, alongside the regional roll out of major national programmes Spaces for Sport & the Arts and Creative Partnerships, working with clusters of local authorities sub-regionally. It is a time I look back on with great joy as a professional and great pride as a colleague.

In life, I believe you can grow where you are planted. I was planted in Oxfordshire through family circumstance in 2004 and KindleCo was born. Two gorgeous children have followed and what a time this has been!

#Co Production is now a personal & professional watchword.

It goes rather nicely with the spirit of #Collaboration, which given the Orchestral starting point, brings me full circle!

I bring a unique blend of skills to what I do.

I help organisations to collaborate, where this is relevant & can add value to their offer. I am experienced in the nurture of new projects, effective in transitions and understand the dynamics of complex Stakeholder relations when working in Government-influenced  environment.

Enabling others to experience the value of creating sound, well-informed #Strategy as part of wider #Organisational Development, is a great personal motivator for me. 

I work through KindleCo mostly as a single Consultant or Project Lead. Small teams of associates can be arranged where needed. I am not a communications, PR or marketing agency but work with you to align these functions, within your overall strategy.

I welcome informal and exploratory chats. You can also Tweet me!
