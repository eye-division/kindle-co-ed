"we are living in times of Revolutionary change....for which there are no precedents...our children are confronting challenges that are unique in human history"
Sir Ken Robinson

Established in 2004, KindleCo is a specialist and  independent service whose mission is to 'support the supporters' of Children, Young People & Families. It is impact-driven and values-led.

Through KindleCo I strive to build capacity & capability in a landscape of change in organisations, strategic partnerships and their associated professionals, including those developing mixed-disciplinary and cross-sector projects funded by public money. I deliver and support skills development in: 

  Organisational development - strategy creation, planning & implementation

  Policy response - knowledge of the external landscape, insight & shaping 

  Project Management - robust oversight & management of multi-party  initiatives

  Partnerships & Resource development - brokerage, positioning, funding & collaborations

  Systems thinking - analysis , design & expression 

 

I share experience built over more than 25 years of work with young people and in brokering, developing and designing multi-organisation programmes which bring mixed- disciplinary and cross-agency teams together. Influencing the agenda of decision makers is integral to that.
For a deeper dive into my qualifications & training please visit my LinkedIn page.
 
My values are to work with the four enduring principles of

Collaboration    Compassion    Courage    Creativity

I strive to bring knowledge, ideas, insights and practical solutions to the table which help make our work together policy-informed & practice-sensitive

I am at my most professionally passionate when working in key Public Policy areas of              Social Inclusion    Learning    Mental Health    Arts

I am passionate about the value of collaboration in Social Purpose work, a committed advocate for equality of opportunity, lover of systems-change thinking, a believer in the transformative power of creativity

On this site you will find a pick & mix of project profiles, client case studies and professional history which seek to reflect this approach and those values.

Expertise

I have worked directly with children, young people and families                 in public sector project development and through strategic partnerships at agency level for over 25 years. Now I deliver my contribution to the sector through Consultancy, Coaching, Advice Surgeries, and through Project Management.  I facilitate & enable Organisations to better understand the landscape external to their projects, to develop their portfolio of work strategically and collaborate with others who can share their purpose and scale impact.

 

A little more about Jo ....

My commitment to developing the skills, talents & lifechances of Children & Young People is longstanding, along with my belief in advocacy as an agent of change.

Following a decade or so working on large scale and infrastructure projects for young people routed through Cultural Services and the Creative Industries, I have focused my attention in more recent years on work which develops provision and infrastructure around Families (including Learning and those who are vulnerable), Mental Health and SEND. Creativity and the Arts remain close to my heart. 

I am a fan of the role of advocacy as as force for positive good, and of Coaching. Alongside organisational Consultancy and Project Management, these form the planks of my current professional offer.

I hold a Certificate from EMCC in Supervision of Consultants, Coaches and Mentors in the workplace.

At practice-level, I originally trained as a Music Practitioner graduating University of Surrey in Orchestral Conducting. It sounds an odd connection to what I do now, granted, but actually those disciplines have much in common - discipline, team work, coaching skills and creating a 'bigger picture' greater than the sum of the parts. Orchestras are also communities and where compassion, respect and diversity are the lifeblood. The move from practitioner to strategist in the Public Sector began for me back in the 1990s when I moved to a Local Authority on Merseyside to work a Family Learning team, promoting the lifechances of vulnerable families. In one guise or another, I have not left since.
 
Despite its difficult challenges, the Public Sector - and its network of partners in the Charitable, Social Enterprise and Voluntary sectors- remains a vibrant place to work with the most committed professionals you will ever meet. The way we do business and interact has radically altered but the dynamics of change, influencing, collaboration and of shared purpose are still built on the quality of Relationships. We have an opportunity every day to make a direct & positive impact on the lifechances of Children & Young People......

Through KindleCo I deliver mostly as a single Consultant or Project Lead to government-funded organisations and their key partners

Read more about KindleCo's work since its inception in 2004 including a large-scale regional contract focused on programme re-design and development of sector partnerships; single locality and sub-regional consortia projects; public report-writing. I have established some long term consultancy relationships including with a national Charity for Vulnerable Families. Coaching is offered specific to public sector and related professionals, especially those taking on a new multi-agency role, Partnerships remit, or who are undergoing a workplace transition.
