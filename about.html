"we are living in times of Revolutionary change....for which there are no precedents...our children are confronting challenges that are unique in human history"

(Sir Ken Robinson, Out of Our Minds, 3rd Edition, 2017)

For the Public Sector & its partners in Social Purpose, the job of looking ahead, understanding the external landscape, responding to change and moving ahead, has never been more challenging

The nature, pace and scope of current societal change is eye-watering. In business environments influenced by public policy, it can be overwhelming.

But it can also be inspiring and full of opportunity.

 

KindleCo exists to help Public Sector & Social Purpose organisations meet these challenges to build perspective, create strategy, grow capability, to galvanise action.

Jo specialises in enabling organisations to find ways to turn external change into a driver for their own strategic development, working with four enduring principles: -

Collaboration, Compassion, Courage & Creativity

I'd like to invite you on a journey ....to nurture the meaning, resilience & impact in your work which will strengthen the future outcomes for your beneficiaries and your contribution to systems-change ...

I believe we all have the opportunity to make a positive impact on the lifechances of Children & Young People whether it is through cultivating stakeholders, brokering collaboration, galvanising political will or and unlocking investment (influence is not always counted in money).

With skills honed over more than 25 years, I facilitate, support & enable those working in a Government - influenced business environment, to develop themselves strategically and find opportunity in working with others. 

I aim to build self-efficacy & capability at organisational, team & individual level through:

 Policy response, insight & shaping 

 Strategy creation 

   Brokerage 

 Systems thinking, planning & expression

 Evaluation

I share a passion for stakeholder dynamics and the key Policy areas -

Social Inclusion, Lifelong Learning (Education & Skills), Mental Health & Wellbeing, Creativity & the Arts

Expertise

Through KindleCo, I share experience gained over the more than 25 years of working in, with & through the Public Sector

If you enjoy a little bit of professional history, you can mine here ....or you can jump straight to my 2018 Services Flyer 

I originally trained as a Musician and grew up in the English Music Conservatoire system. It was a place for all, socially -speaking. I spent many years directly coaching children, young people (and some adults) in schools, colleges, in extended services settings and privately, building their capacity & capability for public performance. 

After teaching for a while, I furthered my passion for team work with a degree in Conducting (yes the baton-waving type, only I never used one of those...) which I appreciate, can seem an odd qualification for a later career in Public Sector Projects &  O.D.

But those disciplines are very closely aligned - success depends on getting the best out of people, cultivating shared endeavour, structure behind the scenes, and creating something together which is so much greater than the sum of the individual parts.....

Working alongside the Public Sector in a strategic capacity, and as part of a Partnership effort, began for me in earnest in 1998. In one guise or another, I have not left since.

Despite its difficult challenges - all the clammer about funding levels - the Public Sector remains a vibrant place to work, with the most committed professionals you will ever meet.

You have the privileged opportunity every day, of making a direct & positive impact, on the lifechances of Children & Young People.

I work now through KindleCo mostly as a single Consultant or Project Lead. Small teams of associates can be arranged where needed.

What I don't do: I am not a communications, PR or marketing agency. But I will work with you to align these functions, within your overall organisational, partnership, project or personal strategy.

I welcome informal and exploratory chats. You can also Tweet me!
