Established in 2004 by Jo Broad, KindleCo is a specialist  independent service 'supporting the supporters' of Children, Young People & Families to thrive in a landscape of change ....

"we are living in times of Revolutionary change....for which there are no precedents...our children are confronting challenges that are unique in human history" (Sir Ken Robinson, Out of Our Minds, 2018)

My work is impact-driven and values-led 

Collaboration   Compassion    Courage    Creativity

 

What work can we do together ?

KNOW, understand, navigate, shape your external landscape through:

 Policy response & Strategy development - reconciling national context with local conditions

  Organisational & Project development -  for public policy, government-influenced environments

Partnerships development - strategic collaborations, partnerships with purpose

 

ENABLE your Team members, Partnership leads & Stakeholders through:

  Projects support- design, direction, management and through coaching  

  Systems thinking - bring sector-level analysis & ideas to the table to co- design 

 

SUSTAIN your organisation, project, partners & purpose through:

  Resource development - design workstreams for the 'bigger picture', position, broker, attract resources

What will the outcomes be ?

Alongside delivering any specific task objectives, I strive to build Knowledge, Confidence, Capacity & Capability. A rounded 360 view of resource development is required in today's fiscal climate. So our discussions will include success criteria on strategic partnerships, positioning, sphere of influence, stakeholders, as well as funding support & advice

 

Who do I work with ? 

Organisations working in partnership-driven, public policy - influenced environments

Project teams developing interventions destined for the social, health or education policy eco-system

Partnerships sited within (or connected to) the Public Sector, and their associated Stakeholder groups

Public Sector & Social Purpose professionals preparing to contribute to the above

Individuals preparing publications and career events including Carers of #SEND & return to work.

 

What might the Tasks be ?

Planning, mapping, plotting, building strategy

Cultivating engagement  - partnerships & stakeholder relations

Influencing - advocacy around a specific Agenda point

Building - opportunity-spotting , developing business for sustainability purposes

Starting & Exiting - project advice & guidance

Leading & Managing - projects with diverse stakeholder needs

Enabling - Coaching projects teams, stakeholder groups: galvanize action around a specific action point

 

What will we have in common?

A shared passion, need and / or commitment to delivering against a canvass of public policy, in a mixed economy of providers, under diverse political leadership, for end-beneficiaries of Children, Young People & Families

I share experience built over more than 25 years of work with young people themselves, and in the brokerage, development and design of partnership-led programmes

Top Tip: your commission does not necessarily have to be largescale, for us to work together!

What makes KindleCo different to other Consultancies?  I strive to bring knowledge, insights and ideas borne out of experience at the coal face of practice and project delivery, to the table. I will work with you, your team members, your partners and stakeholders to develop practical solutions to current challenges, help you anticipate future ones, navigate the unfamiliar and identify your best fit opportunities going forward.

My input will be policy-informed & practice-sensitive when your work delivers opportunities to your end-beneficiaries for Learning, Skills development, Social Inclusion & Mobility, Wellbeing (including Mental Health) and Creativity and promotes the lifechances of                                    Children, Young People & Families

Expertise

I have worked directly with children, young people & families and with the public sector in project development and strategic partnerships for over 25 years...

A little more about Jo ....

My commitment to developing the skills and #lifechances of Children & Young People is longstanding, along with my belief in #advocacy and #systems-thinking as agents of change.

After a period working through the Cultural Services & Creative Industries on projects at local, sub-regional and regional agency levels, I made a move to local government in the 1990s as part of a dynamic Family Learning Team serving some of the most deprived and vulnerable families. I expanded my practice skills in order to be able to support organisations delivering within complex stakeholder environments on the agendas for #Learning, #Skills Development, #Wellbeing, #Vulnerable children, #Mental Health & #SEND. #Creativity and the #Arts remain close to my professional heart. I am also passionate about #innovation in public services
