Established in 2004 by Jo Broad, KindleCo is a specialist  independent service 'supporting the supporters' of Children, Young People & Families to thrive in a landscape of change ....

"we are living in times of Revolutionary change....for which there are no precedents...our children are confronting challenges that are unique in human history" (Sir Ken Robinson, Out of Our Minds, 2018)

My work is impact-driven and values-led 

Collaboration   Compassion    Courage    Creativity

 

What do I deliver ?

  Organisational development - creation & implementation of strategy

  Policy & Systems - knowing, understanding, shaping & deploying your external landscape  

  Projects - planning, direction & coaching with multiple stakeholders 

  Partnerships & Resources - development, brokerage, positioning, funding, collaborating

  Systems - thinking, analysing, co- designing

What outcomes will there be ?

I strive to build three things: confidence, capacity, capability

Who do I work with ? 

Its diverse but includes Organisations working in partnership- driven environments; Project teams, their Stakeholders; Networks & Consortia; individuals

What kind of tasks do I take on?

You may need: to know your external landscape, process it and plan strategically

Seek greater or different engagement with others - partners and stakeholders

Use systems to advocate & influence or advocate specific Agenda points

Opportunity-spot or develop business for sustainability

Set up a Project, roll out, manage or exit

Prepare a workplace transition, or Career event and be supported via Coaching

 

What will we have in common?

A shared passion, need or commitment to delivering against a canvass of public policy, often with Government-funding as part of a mixed economy, under diverse political persuasions

I share experience built over more than 25 years of work with young people themselves, and in the brokerage, development and design of partnership-led programmes

 

Qualifications & training examples:

Practitioner: LTCL; Essentials for Supervision of Coaches, Mentors & Consultants (EMCC); Safeguarding Children & Young People Level 3 (LCSB); Resilience - Ordinary Magic & Learnable Skills (Young Minds)
Workplace: Leadership at Work (Bourton Group); Financial Management in the Public Sector (Directory of Social Change)

What makes KindleCo different to other Consultancies?  I strive to bring knowledge, insights and ideas borne out of experience at the coal face of practice and project delivery, to the table. I will work with you, your team members, your partners and stakeholders to develop practical solutions to current challenges, help you anticipate future ones, navigate the unfamiliar and identify your best fit opportunities going forward.

My input will be policy-informed & practice-sensitive when your work delivers opportunities to your end-beneficiaries for Learning, Skills development, Social Inclusion & Mobility, Wellbeing (including Mental Health) and Creativity and promotes the lifechances of                                    Children, Young People & Families

I am committed to the value of collaboration in Social Purpose work, an advocate for equality of opportunity, a lover of systems-change thinking and a believer in the transformative power of creativity

This site is a pick & mix of project profiles, client case studies and professional history which seek to reflect this approach and those values.

Expertise

I have worked directly with children, young people & families and with the public sector in project development and strategic partnerships for over 25 years...

A little more about Jo ....

My commitment to developing the skills and lifechances of Children & Young People is longstanding, along with my belief in advocacy and systems-thinking as agents of change. After a period working through the Cultural Services & Creative Industries on projects at local, sub-regional and regional agency levels, I expanded my practice skills in order to be able to support organisations delivering within complex stakeholder environments on the agendas for Learning, Skills Development, Wellbeing, Vulnerable children, Mental Health & SEND. Creativity and the Arts remain close to my professional heart.

I made a move to local government in the 1990s as part of a dynamic Family Learning Team serving some of the most deprived, promoting the lifechances of some of our most vulnerable families. In one way or another, I have not departed the public sector scene since although much of my more recent work sits alongside it, rather than within. It and its network of partners in the Charity, Social Enterprise and Voluntary sectors, make it a vibrant place to engage with the most committed professionals you will ever meet.
 
The way we do business in that time has radically altered, but the dynamics of change, influence and shared purpose are still built on the same stuff: the quality of system-thinking and of Relationships.  

I originally trained as a Music Practitioner.  It sounds an odd connection to what I do now, granted, but actually those disciplines have much in common - team work, coaching for performance, knowing the internal and external environment. Making the 'bigger picture' more than the sum of its individual parts. Orchestras are communities where hard work, skill, compassion, respect and diversity are the lifeblood. Collaboration matters.
