Established in 2004 by Jo Broad, KindleCo is a specialist  independent service 'supporting the supporters' of Children, Young People & Families to thrive in a landscape of change ....

This site acts as a simple on-line portfolio for my professional work. I welcome informal approaches and am happy to discuss your project, professional or organisational needs in a no-obligation exploratory call. If you'd like to learn a little more about me and the work we might do together, read on.

Alternatively, skip to My Services or download here my Services Flyer

"we are living in times of Revolutionary change....for which there are no precedents...our children are confronting challenges that are unique in human history" (Sir Ken Robinson, Out of Our Minds, 2018)

 

I am committed to the value of collaboration in Social Purpose work, advocate for equality of opportunity, believer in compassion-led design, lover of systems-change thinking and witness to the transformative power of creativity

Through KindleCo, I share experience built over more than 25 years of work with young people, families and the organisations that support them. I have led the design & development of strategic programmes and cross-boundary projects in the public sector, supported a range of private clients from education charities to public agencies, and enabled individual projects & professionals to reach their goals.

I specialise in partnering those delivering against a backdrop of public policy

 who are driven by social purpose

Your project does not have to be big for us to work together!

My work is impact-driven and values-led 

"Collaboration   Compassion    Courage    Creativity"

 

A little more about Jo ....

My commitment to developing the skills and lifechances of Children & Young People is longstanding, along with my belief in advocacy and systems-thinking as agents of change

After a period working through the Cultural Services & Creative Industries on multi-agency projects at local, sub-regional and regional agency levels, I have expanded my practice skills in order to be able to support organisations delivering within complex stakeholder environments on the agendas for Wellbeing, Learning and Vulnerable children.

I am a qualified Mental Health First-Aider (adult) with a passion for Workforce Development

A committed contributor to Co Production, I have worked in local government in Family Learning serving some of the most deprived and vulnerable families of the UK.  I have worked with a multitude LAs both singly and as part of regional partnerships spanning locations Greater Manchester, Merseyside, London to East England

I have led strategic partnerships on behalf of Local Authorities and held responsibility for a regional portfolio with Arts Council England. I have brokered cross-sector and mixed-disciplinary partnerships between public sector, community-led organisations and private sector organisations with corporate social responsibility programmes influencing decision-makers to create provision and resources to benefit Children & Young People through collaboration

I understand the need for sustainability to remain centre-stage in Organisational Development and in sector-level change.

I am able to share with clients my experience of winning bids for KindleCo contracts through competitive tendering, of leading successful funding applications to regional programmes on behalf of public agency partnerships, of building successful submissions to Government and European funding pots, such as the Standards Fund, Lottery and delegated ERDF. 

Although I do not undertake bid-writing as a stand-alone task, clients can be sure I bring to the table a skills-set that allows me to provide well-informed support to clients who must focus future-proofing. I will help you ensure your strategic plans take care of the resources, language and partnerships which can promote your sustainability. 

I now also Coach professionals working in and alongside the Public Sector in Education and other social purpose settings, to enable successful workplace transitions including return to work for #carers.

Here are thoughts about Working Together

What will we have in common?

A shared passion, need and / or commitment to delivering against a canvass of public policy, in a mixed economy of providers, under diverse political leadership, for end-beneficiaries of Children, Young People & Families

What work could we do together ?  In a nutshell.... 

  • deepen an understanding of your external environment, navigate and shape it through policy-response & strategy development
  • build meaningful alliances around your purpose through stakeholder engagement and strategic partnerships 
  • deploy external change as a driver for your own project or organisational development

What would the outcomes be?

Every piece of work & every community is different. All my consultancy is bespoke. But alongside  specific task objectives, I strive to build:

  • Knowledge, Confidence, Capacity & Capability
  • A rounded 360 view of resource development: this is a "must" in today's fiscal climate,  so expect our discussions to include developing objectives around strategic partnerships, sector positioning, and stakeholders, as well as funding
