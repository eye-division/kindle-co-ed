BACKSTORY

A little more about Jo .... my commitment to developing the lifechances of Children & Young People is longstanding

After a period working with Arts, Cultural Services & Creative Industries on Government funded projects for Children & Young People at locality and regional - agency level, I expanded my professional practice skills to include specific support for organisations with end-beneficiaries of Vulnerable Families . I now engage in a wider menu of activities with sector champions for Wellbeing, Learning, Creativity and Education.

I am a qualified Adult Mental Health First-Aider.

As a committed advocate for Co Production, I have worked in local government in Family Learning serving some of the most deprived and vulnerable families of the UK.  I have worked with a critical mass of diverse Local Authorities in locations spanning Merseyside, Greater Manchester, London and East England either singly, or as consortia in strategic roles, driving a number of regional partnerships. I have led strategic intervention work with public agencies including a regional portfolio with Arts Council England, leading the regional roll-out of national programmes with Schools.  

Brokering cross-boundary partnerships between organisations in the past, has given me a sound grasp of the dynamics of mixed-disciplinary work.

I originally trained as a Music Practitioner training children & young people (and a few adults for good measure) in group music practice. Having been raised, creatively-speaking, in the English conservatoire system as a child , studying at both the Guildhall and Royal Academy of Music junior departments, I later took up conducting completing my Honours Degree in performance at the University of Surrey. Children & Young People, what and how they learn and the bigger societal picture, have never been very far from my mind throughout. Not many people did a research project on 'Systematic methods of String Teaching and Early Childhood Education' when I was at University!  It all  sounds an odd connection to what I do now, but actually, the process of bringing diverse stakeholders  together around common purpose and group music practice, are disciplines with much to learn from each other: team work, coaching, knowing your internal and external landscapes, embracing harmony and dissonance, delivering for a shared mission.... Orchestras are essentially one big learning community: hard work, a focus on skills-building, compassion, mutual respect and diversity are the lifeblood - collaboration is 'do or die', artistically and socially.
 
The big picture really is, more than the sum, of the individual parts.
 
 

IMPACT

Successfully influencing decision-makers to create provision and resources to benefit Children & Young People, through collaboration with others, is part of my track record and I firmly believe 'Collaboration' is a value and a behaviour we choose to adopt, not just a process exclusively reserved for those operating at scale.

I also understand the need for sustainability to remain centre-stage in Organisational Development and in sector-level change.

I am able to share with clients my experience of winning bids for KindleCo contracts through competitive tendering, of leading successful funding applications to regional programmes on behalf of public agency partnerships, of building successful submissions to Government and European funding pots, such as the Standards Fund, Lottery and delegated ERDF.

Although I do not undertake bid-writing as a stand-alone task, you can be sure I bring to the table experience which enables me to provide well-informed support to clients, in an age where future-proofing is key. I will help you ensure your strategic plans take care of the resources, language and strategic partnerships which can promote your sustainability.

I now also Coach professionals working in and alongside the Public Sector in Education and other social purpose settings, to enable successful workplace transitions. I love bringing a combination of lived and professional experience, to supporting #carers returning to work.

I undertake a small amount of Social Responsibility activity each year, despite being a micro-enterprise myself, and currently living my values through active participation as a member of the National Children's Bureau's Family Research Advisory Group

You can learn more about the outcomes of my work by visiting       Case Studies
