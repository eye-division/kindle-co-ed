Through KindleCo, I share experience built over 25 years in public sector project design, development of strategic partnerships, nurture of stakeholder initiatives and  management of multi-agency programmes.

I am passionate about the value of collaboration in Social Purpose work, a committed advocate for equality of opportunity, lover of systems-change thinking and believe in the transformative power of creativity.

Dotted around this website are images which in one way or another, reflect those values.

KindleCo specialises in supporting our sector champions in the key Public Policy areas of Social Inclusion, Lifelong Learning (Education & Skills), Mental Health and the Arts in their endeavours, specifically - 

Organisational development, the growth of collaborative Partnerships, the shaping of Projects as strategic interventions and leading Programme roll out.

I strive to enable the organisations & individuals who keep the Children & Families sector afloat, to turn external change, into a driver for their own strategic development.

Working with my four enduring principles -

Collaboration, Compassion, Courage & Creativity 

KindleCo exists to enable our sector champions to take forward their ambitions:

 by growing perspective, creating strategy, nurturing capacity and galvanising action

Your work is likely to be delivered in a setting which is mixed-disciplinary or multi-agency in nature and seeks outcomes which help build infrastructure in your sector, organisation, team or individuals for the ultimate benefit of Children, YP & Families.

A little bit about Jo

My commitment to developing the skills, talents & lifechances of Children & Young People is longstanding, along with my belief in advocacy as an agent of change. I started my first after school club when I was 10 and was reminded recently, that I sent my first letter to a Politician about family-friendly policies, not long before.

I originally trained as a Musician and grew up educationally, in the English state school & Music Conservatoire systems. I found these both to be 'places for all', socially -speaking. 

Professionally, I have spent many years coaching children, young people (and some adults) in schools, colleges, in extended services settings and privately, building their capacity & capability for group practice and public performance.  I graduated from University of Surrey with a degree in Conducting (yes the baton-waving type, only I never used one of those...) and with a dollop of Research into musical literacy as a vehicle for early childhood development.

I recognise, this can seem an odd set of qualifications for a later career in developing Public Sector projects and  O.D.

But those disciplines are very closely aligned - success depends on getting the best out of people, cultivating shared endeavour, design-thinking building structure behind the scenes and most of all, creating an experience collaboratively where the outcome is far greater than the sum of the individual parts.....

Picture featured by kind permission of One Stage

Whilst I don't regularly practice in the music field now, I have retained my contact with group professional practice and a commitment to nurturing the lifechances of Children & Young People. CPD in more recent years has developed my own practitioner skills in work intersecting Mental Health, Vulnerable Families and SEND along with agency-sensitive qualifications in Safeguarding, Inter- Agency working, Mentoring & Supervision.

Working alongside the Public Sector in a strategic capacity began for me back in the 1990s in the Cultural Sector and in one guise or another, I have not left since.

Despite its difficult challenges, the Public Sector - and its network of partners in the Charitable, Social Enterprise and Voluntary sectors- remains a vibrant place to work with the most committed professionals you will ever meet.

And whilst the way we do business and interact has radically altered in that time, the dynamics of change, influence and shared purpose have not: they are still built on the quality of Relationships

We have an opportunity every day to make a direct & positive impact on the lifechances of Children & Young People

 

Through KindleCo, I now deliver mostly as a single Consultant or Project Lead to government-funded organisations and their key partners.

Since 2004, commissions have included a large-scale regional brief to model the spin-out of a Government-run initiative in Education, developing sector partnerships in a single locality with individual Learning providers and with sub-regional Consortia. I have assisted in the preparation of editorial for peer-reviewed articles and led public report-writing. KindleCo also maintains Consultancy relationships over the longterm with clients, including a national Charity at the cutting edge of innovation in the support of Vulnerable Families. In 2018, I have made a direct return to individual Coaching, working with professionals undergoing a workplace transition
