"we are living in times of Revolutionary change....a global revolution....for which there are no precedents...our children are confronting challenges that are unique in human history"

(Sir Ken Robinson, Out of Our Minds, 3rd Edition, 2017)

The job of looking ahead, understanding the external environment, preparing for change and moving ahead has never been more challenging for the Public Sector & its Social Purpose partners....

The nature, pace and scope of societal change is eye-watering. Experienced in a Government-influenced working environment, it can be overwhelming but also inspiring.
It can also be full of opportunity.
Founded by Jo Broad in 2004, KindleCo is a specialist partner to Public Sector & Social Purpose organisations occupied with sector-level change.

Jo specialises in enabling organisations, and individual professionals, to find creative, meaningful and pragmatic ways to turn external change into a driver for their own strategic development.

Through KindleCo, Jo commits to work which is values - led and outcomes-driven, with my four professional watchwords:

Collaboration   Compassion   Courage  &  Creativity

I'd like to invite you on a journey to build greater meaning, resilience and impact into your work, strengthen the outcomes for your beneficiaries and your contribution to systems-change" 
Whether it is winning stakeholder buy-in, enabling inter-agency collaboration, galvanising political will or unlocking investment, we all have the opportunity to make an impact for Children & Young People.

With skills honed over more than  25 years of working directly with Children & Young People and with the agencies which champion them, Jo facilitates, supports & enables 

Organisations , Strategic Partnerships , Networks  & Consortia  

working in a Government - influenced business environment to develop, collaborate and evaluate -  to respond confidently to external change by building their own capacities for:

Policy insight

Strategy creation 

Collaboration 

Systems thinking & expression

Reflection in evaluation

Working in the key Public Policy areas of

Social Inclusion, Lifelong Learning, Education & Skills, Mental Health & Wellbeing, Creativity & the Arts

Expertise

Through KindleCo, I share experience gained over the past 25+ years of working in & with the Public Sector at local, regional and sector level

I understand the challenges and the joys of

inter-agency working, stringent funding criteria, complex stakeholder dynamics

I trained initially as a Musician, spending many years coaching children, young people & adults in schools, extended service settings and privately, building their capacity & capability for public performance. I hold a BMus. Hons in Conducting which seems an odd qualification for strategy and organisational development.

But those disciplines are very closely aligned. They pivot on getting the best out of people, and unlocking their potential to collaborate to create a bigger picture.

I entered the realms of  working alongside the Public Sector in 1998 when I took on the artistic programming and business development of a community-led venture in West London, developing its first ever Outreach Programme for Arts & Community Education.

It wasn't long before I was bitten by the multi-agency working bug, prompting a move to St Helens Council in Merseyside in 1998 to be their first ever Arts Officer.

Boyed on by that Local Authority's incredible commitment to Learning as a tool for Social Inclusion,  Prosperity & Mobility, I established the towns first ever cross-agency Cultural Partnership and with it, a first Creative Industries business incubation programme for Young People. There followed a new Youth Theatre, Disability Arts Programme and successful bids to the government's Standards Fund, Arts Council and a first dive into ERDF, helping to light the way.

I delivered a stint at Arts Council England (as their Regional Officer for Lifelong Learning & Skills) in the East Region in the early 2000s. Tasked with leading the construction of ACE East's new three-year regional Learning & Skills strategy, and the regional roll out of our national programmes Spaces for Sport & the Arts and Creative Partnerships, put me to work alongside large clusters of local authorities.

A particularly driven CEO, with a robust commitment to in-house CPD and ambition for the Region, enabled me to forge a first corporate cross-sector partnership between ACE, BAA and the then- LSC, forming a Workforce development programme with Local Authorities and FE Providers, using the Arts as a delivery mechanism with NEETs.

That saw me through my first direct experience, of a major national public agency restructure. 

In life, I believe you must grow where you are planted. I was planted in Oxfordshire through family circumstance in 2004 and KindleCo was born.

Two gorgeous children have followed and what a time this has been! Co Production is now a personal & professional watchword.

It does go rather nicely with Collaboration, which given the Orchestral starting point, brings me full circle.

I bring a unique blend of skills to what I do.

I work through KindleCo mostly as a single Consultant or Project Lead. Small teams of associates can be arranged where needed. I am not a communications, PR or marketing agency but work with you to align these functions, within your overall strategy.

I welcome informal and exploratory chats. You can also Tweet me!
