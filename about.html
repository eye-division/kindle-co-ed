Established in 2004 by Jo Broad, KindleCo is a specialist  independent service 'supporting the supporters' of Children, Young People & Families to thrive in a landscape of change ....

"we are living in times of Revolutionary change....for which there are no precedents...our children are confronting challenges that are unique in human history" (Sir Ken Robinson, Out of Our Minds, 2018)

 

I am committed to the value of collaboration in Social Purpose work, an advocate for equality of opportunity, a lover of systems-change thinking and a believer in the transformative power of creativity

Through KindleCo, I share experience built over more than 25 years of work with young people themselves, in the design & development of strategic partnerships with public sector agencies and in the implementation of government-funded, multi-agency programmes 

My work is impact-driven and values-led 

"Collaboration   Compassion    Courage    Creativity"

This site is an online portfolio 

It offers a simple pick & mix of project profiles, client case studies and professional history

A little more about Jo ....

My commitment to developing the skills and lifechances of Children & Young People is longstanding, along with my belief in advocacy and systems-thinking as agents of change

After a period working through the Cultural Services & Creative Industries on multi-agency projects at local, sub-regional and regional agency levels, I have expanded my practice skills in order to be able to support organisations delivering within complex stakeholder environments on the agendas for Wellbeing, Vulnerable children, Mental Health & SEND.

I am a committed contributor to Co Production. I have worked in local government in Family Learning serving some of the most deprived and vulnerable families of the UK. In addition to my earlier teaching practice, I have worked with around 50 LAs both singly and as part of regional partnerships spanning locations Greater Manchester, Merseyside, London, rural East England and Essex.

I have led strategic partnerships for St Helens Council including as part of regional finding programmes, held responsibility for a regional portfolio with Arts Council England, and brokered cross-sector and mixed-disciplinary partnerships between public sector, community-led organisations and corporate social responsibility programmes to support Children, Young People & Families. 

Under the KindleCo banner I have successful bid for contracts from public agencies and cultivated my own clients, both organisations and individuals.

If your wondering if I would provide a good fit with your organisation and its partners, here are some helpful tips about Working Together:

What will we have in common?

A shared passion, need and / or commitment to delivering against a canvass of public policy, in a mixed economy of providers, under diverse political leadership, for end-beneficiaries of Children, Young People & Families

What work could we do together ?  In a nutshell.... 

  • deepen an understanding of your external environment, navigate and shape it through policy-response & strategy development
  • build meaningful alliances around your purpose through stakeholder engagement and strategic partnerships 
  • deploy external change as a driver for your own project or organisational development

What would the outcomes be?

Every piece of work & every community is different. All my consultancy is bespoke. But alongside  specific task objectives, I strive to build:

  • Knowledge, Confidence, Capacity & Capability
  • A rounded 360 view of resource development: this is a "must" in today's fiscal climate,  so expect our discussions to include developing objectives around strategic partnerships, sector positioning, and stakeholders, as well as funding

Top Tip: your commission does not necessarily have to be largescale, for us to work together!
